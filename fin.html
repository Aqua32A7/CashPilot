✦ FAQs

Questions founders
actually ask

Everything you need to know about CashPilot and the runway calculator.

What exactly is "runway" and why does it matter?
+

Runway is the number of months your startup can operate before running out of cash, assuming your current burn rate stays constant. It's one of the most critical metrics for any founder — it tells you how much time you have to reach the next milestone, raise funds, or hit profitability. Investors always ask about it, and you should always know the answer.

How is net burn different from gross burn?
+

Gross burn is your total monthly cash spend — salaries, rent, SaaS tools, everything out the door. Net burn subtracts any revenue you bring in. So if you spend ₹5L/mo but earn ₹1.2L/mo, your net burn is ₹3.8L/mo. The calculator uses net burn for a more accurate runway picture. Enter your MRR in the "Advanced" section to factor it in.

When should I start fundraising according to the zones?
+

The rule of thumb: start fundraising when you hit the Yellow (Caution) Zone — 6 to 11 months of runway. Enterprise fundraising rounds take 3–6 months on average, so waiting until you're in the Danger Zone (<6 months) puts you in a weak negotiating position. Ideally, you should begin conversations with investors while you're still in the Safe Zone (12+ months).

Does the calculator account for revenue growth over time?
+

Yes! Use the growth rate slider in the Advanced section to model burn that compounds monthly. A positive growth rate means your net burn increases each month (useful for scaling teams), while a negative rate models burn reduction scenarios. This makes the projections far more realistic than a flat cash ÷ burn formula.

Is my financial data saved anywhere?
+

No. The CashPilot runway calculator runs entirely in your browser. We don't send any numbers you enter to our servers — there's nothing to log, store, or breach. Your data stays on your device. This is intentional: founders deserve tools that respect confidentiality without needing to create an account.

What's included in "cash on hand" — should I count credit lines?
+

Cash on hand should reflect liquid, immediately accessible funds — typically your current bank balance(s) and any fixed deposits you could break within a week. Be conservative. Don't include undrawn credit lines, pending customer invoices, or committed-but-not-wired investor money. Overestimating cash is a common mistake that gives a false sense of security.

Can I use this for Series A and beyond, or just early-stage?
+

Absolutely — the calculator works at any stage. We've included pre-built scenarios for Early Stage SaaS, Series A Growth, and Critical Zone situations so you can see how different funding stages look. Whether you're a bootstrapped team of 3 or a 50-person post-Series A company, runway planning never stops being relevant.

How is CashPilot different from a simple spreadsheet?
+

Spreadsheets require you to build and maintain formulas, handle edge cases, and interpret the results yourself. CashPilot gives you instant zone classification, a visual cash decline chart, scenario planning with fundraise timing, and growth-rate compounding — all in one purpose-built interface designed for founders, not accountants. Think of it as your CFO's back-of-envelope, upgraded.
